User Experience Research

S-Kauppa online grocery service

Overview

This UX research project focused on S-Kaupat, an online grocery service in Finland. I wanted to understand real user experiences and find out where things get frustrating. The study looked at payment flow, search accuracy, delivery timing, and pickup pricing. Based on what I learned, I put together a simple personalization concept and some practical ideas to improve the core user experience.

Research Process

I started with a heuristic evaluation of the S-Kaupat interface, then ran a Google Forms survey that got 31 responses. After that, I did three in-depth interviews with actual users. I analyzed everything using affinity mapping and created simple personas to capture what matters most to people: saving time, avoiding crowded stores, sticking to their budget, and feeling confident using the service.

Later on, I did a quick feedback round with 5 people to see how they reacted to the concept I proposed.

Project facts

Course: DIG006AS2A, Digital Product Project

School: Haaga-Helia University of Applied Sciences

Timeline: About 6 weeks in late 2023

Team: Just me on this one

My role: UX research, analysis, synthesis, concept framing

Goal: Find usability problems and propose user-centered improvements for S-Kaupat

Methods: Heuristic evaluation, online survey (31 responses), user interviews (3 people), affinity mapping, personas

What I delivered: Research summary, affinity map, personas, concept description

Impact

The research made it clear what users actually need from the service. It helped prioritize improvements in payment reliability, search functionality, and delivery transparency. Most importantly, it gave S-Kaupat a solid foundation for making the experience more convenient and trustworthy.
