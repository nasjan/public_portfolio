User Experience Research — S-Kaupat

S-kauppa

Overview

UX research project focused on the S-Kaupat online grocery service in Finland. The study explored real user experiences and identified key pain points in payment flow, search accuracy, delivery timing, and pickup pricing. The insights guided a low-effort opt-in personalisation concept and practical improvement ideas for core user flows.

Research Process

The research included a heuristic evaluation of the S-Kaupat interface, a Google Forms survey (n = 31), and three semi-structured user interviews. Responses were analysed through affinity mapping and simplified persona sketches to summarise user motivations: saving time, avoiding crowded stores, staying within budget, and trusting the digital service.

A small feedback round (n = 5) was later used to collect early reactions to the proposed concept.

Project facts

Course: DIG006AS2A — Digital Product Project

Institution: Haaga-Helia University of Applied Sciences

Timeframe: ~6 weeks, late 2023

Team: Jan Nässi

Role: UX research; analysis; synthesis; concept framing

Objective: Identify usability issues and propose user-centred improvements for the S-Kaupat online grocery experience

Methods: Heuristic evaluation; online survey (n = 31); user interviews (n = 3); affinity mapping; light personas

Deliverables: Research summary report; affinity map; personas; concept description

Impact

The research clarified user expectations and prioritised improvements in payment reliability, search usability, and delivery transparency. It established a foundation for future S-Kaupat UX enhancements focused on convenience and trust.
